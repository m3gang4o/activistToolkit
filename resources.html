Headlines

Below are reported incidents of hate crimes in the United States.

Atlanta Spa Shootings

Date: March 16, 2021
Location: Atlanta, Georgia

In Atlanta, Georgia, eight people were killed and one wounded at three different spas. Six of the eight people were women of Asian descent. The victims included Suncha Kim, 69; Soon Chung Park, 74; Hyun Jung Grant, 51; and Yong Ae Yue, 63, Paul Michels, 54; Xiaojie "Emily" Tan, 49; Daoyou Feng, 44; and Delaina Yaun, 33. The suspect, Robert Aaron Long (21 years old), claimed that he was driven by his sexual urges instead of racial bias. He was sentenced to four life sentences without parole and an additional 35 years.

Hair World Salon Shooting

Date: May 11, 2022
Location: Dallas, Texas

John Theron Smith (36 years old) entered Hair World Salon located in Dallas Koreatown and began shooting throughout the store. He fired about thirteen times and injured three people. The salon was Korean-owned, and police are investigating the shooting as a potential hate crime. This shooting was also linked to two other Dallas attacks on Asian-owned businesses.

Chinatown Stabbing

Date: February 2022
Location: New York City

Christina Yuna Lee (35 years old) was coming home after a Saturday night out when she began to be followed by Assamad Nash (25 years old). He followed her to her apartment without her knowledge, forced himself inside, and stabbed her dozens of times. Her neighbors heard her screams and called the police. The police found Lee's dead body in her bathroom and Nash hiding underneath her bed.

Woman Shoved In Front of Subway

Date: January 2022
Location: New York City

Michelle Alyssa Go was waiting for the R train at the Times Square station when she was shoved onto the tracks. The suspect was Simon Martial (61 years old), who was homeless. Go had no interaction with Martial before he pushed her. He was charged with second-degree murder. Thousands in New York City and the United States mourned her death.
